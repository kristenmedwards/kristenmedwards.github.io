Kristen M. Edwards

I'm a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at MIT’s DeCoDE Lab, where I research at the intersection of artificial intelligence (AI), engineering design, and manufacturing, with a focus on sustainability and global development. My work combines machine learning, computer vision, and design theory to evaluate and generate innovative product concepts. I'm supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, the Ida M. Green Fellowship, and the GEM Fellowship.

In addition to my academic research, I’ve applied machine learning to real-world challenges at organizations like the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Lab, the National Renewable Energy Lab, and the US Department of Energy. My research has been published in Science Advances, PNAS Nexus, the Journal of Mechanical Design, and Artificial Intelligence Review, and I've presented my work at conferences including ASME IDETC-CIE, CDFAM, AI4EM, and the Design Society's DESIGN. I’m passionate about building inclusive, accessible tools that empower engineers and designers around the world.
